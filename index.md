## About This Book

This textbook is designed for undergraduate and graduate accounting and business students who need a practical introduction to analytics without assuming prior programming experience. The goal is not simply to teach software features. The goal is to help students ask better questions, work effectively with accounting data, and communicate findings in forms that support financial reporting, managerial decision-making, and audit assurance.

The book is built around a single integrated toolkit. Students learn to move from spreadsheet-based analysis in Excel, to direct database access in SQL, to interactive reporting in Power BI. That sequence mirrors how analytics work often unfolds in practice: data is extracted, prepared, analyzed, and then presented to decision makers in a format they can actually use.

## How the Book Is Structured

::: {.intro-card}
### Consistent Chapter Design

Each chapter follows the same general pattern: learning objectives, a motivating scenario, explanatory narrative, guided work, a summary, key terms, review questions, and applied exercises organized by accounting perspective.

::: {.intro-card}

Exercises are grouped into Financial Accounting, Managerial Accounting, and Auditing so that students see how one analytical technique can support multiple professional roles.

The text begins with foundations, moves into Excel, then SQL, then Power BI, and finally integrates the full stack in later chapters and applied cases.

## About the Datasets

This textbook uses three companion datasets in both Excel workbook format and SQLite database format. That means students can work with the same underlying business data across all three tools instead of learning each tool in isolation.

### Northwind Traders

Northwind Traders is a small wholesale food distribution company with a compact, easy-to-understand structure. It includes customers, orders, order details, products, categories, suppliers, employees, and shippers. Because the database is small and transparent, it is the primary dataset for foundational chapters and first exposures to analytical workflows.

### AdventureWorks Cycles

AdventureWorks Cycles is a mid-size multinational bicycle manufacturer with substantial operational complexity. Its data spans sales, production, purchasing, human resources, and organizational structure. It is the main dataset for managerial and cost accounting applications because it supports product costing, production analytics, purchasing evaluation, and multi-territory sales analysis.

### ERPNext Demo Company

ERPNext Demo Company represents a full enterprise resource planning environment with a complete accounting module. It includes a chart of accounts, general ledger entries, journal entries, budgets, cost centers, invoices, and payment records. It is the most accounting-rich dataset in the book and becomes central in financial reporting, audit testing, and integrated analytics work.

::: {.callout-note .connecting-dots icon=false}
## Dataset Progression
The datasets appear in a deliberate sequence. Northwind comes first because it reduces cognitive load. AdventureWorks enters as the analytical tasks become richer. ERPNext is introduced once students are ready to work inside a full accounting system rather than a narrower operational database.
:::

## How to Use This Text

Students can read the chapters linearly, but instructors can also emphasize different sections depending on course goals. In a more introductory course, the emphasis may remain on Chapters 1 through 10 and selected Power BI topics. In a more advanced or graduate course, the later integrated chapters can carry more weight and be paired with deeper case analysis, professional standards, and research discussion.

The book is also structured to support multiple teaching formats. It works for a single semester course, a modular analytics sequence, or a course that selectively emphasizes financial reporting, managerial accounting, or audit analytics.

## What Comes Next

Chapter 1 introduces the role of analytics in accounting, the three types of analytics, the three companion datasets, and the overall workflow that connects the rest of the book. It establishes the conceptual foundation for the technical chapters that follow.
